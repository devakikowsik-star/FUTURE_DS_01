Executive Business Analysis & Strategic Recommendations

Based on the cleaning and analysis of 2025 sales transaction logs, we have identified key revenue-driving areas, profit anomalies, and structural opportunities. The following insights represent a consultative review of business sales performance, indicating where management should focus resources for maximum growth and margin expansion.

1

Technology Dominates Revenue, Furniture Drags Profit Margins

Analysis: Our analysis reveals that Technology products generate over 50% of total company revenue, with products like the Pro Laptop 15 leading in sales volume and unit price. Conversely, the Furniture category generates high transaction volumes but suffers from significantly lower profit margins due to high product acquisition costs (averaging ~60-65% cost ratios).

Action Plan

Optimize the shipping/warehousing cost structure for Furniture items. Reposition Furniture as bundle addons to Technology purchases (e.g., selling standing desks with monitor mounts) to enhance overall basket value.

2

Data Quality Audit & Sales Leakage Resolved

Analysis: During the data engineering stage, our pipeline identified that approximately 3% of entries were duplicates, and multiple sales records lacked valid unit prices or quantity figures. By running the Python imputation algorithm, we resolved pricing gaps (averaging $12-$1200 per product) and filtered out negative quantities, which would have artificially depressed reported profits by thousands of dollars.

Action Plan

Implement POS software restrictions preventing negative quantity inputs, and require product database linkages to automatically fill unit prices at checkout to eliminate manual entry price gaps.

3

High Profitability in Regional Pockets

Analysis: Cross-tabulation of region and profit margin shows that the East and West regions produce the largest overall sales volume, but the North region shows the highest net profit margin percentage (average 42%). This indicates that sales in the North, though lower in total volume, are highly efficient and focused on high-margin accessory items (e.g., USB-C cables, mouse pads, sticky notes).

Action Plan

Shift a portion of the marketing budget from the highly competitive West region to the North region. Run targeted local campaigns to expand customer acquisition in the North, utilizing their high-margin profile to increase overall company profitability.

4

Capitalize on High-Turn, Low-Cost Accessories

Analysis: Office supplies (like Gel Pens and Sticky Notes) have exceptionally low unit prices but exhibit extremely high sales velocities. Because their cost-of-goods-sold (COGS) ratio is minimal (~25-30%), they contribute a steady, risk-free stream of profit with low overhead and capital lock-up compared to big-ticket electronics.

Action Plan

Place high-turn accessory items directly at checkout/main-landing-pages as point-of-sale impulse purchases to increase average order value (AOV) without inventory pressure.
